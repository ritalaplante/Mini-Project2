Graduate Outcomes by College Major

Introduction:

We chose to analyze the dataset "College Majors" from Five Thirty Eight. The dataset contains information on outcomes for various college majors. Select variables include median earnings by major, enrollment by major, employment rate by major, the share of women enrolled in each major, and the major category. We created a series of dashboards to analyze how outcomes differ by college major.

For our data exploration, we chose to analyze the College Majors dataset. The dataset contains the breakdown of different majors across the United States, from the American Community Survey 2010-2012 Public Use Microdata Series. For each major, the dataset includes the major category, total number of people with the major, how many men and women have the major, median earnings of full-time, the unemployment rate, and other statistics breaking down each major. The large number of categories allowed us to investigate the popularity of certain majors across demographic groups and how these correlate to jobs outside of college. We structured our data exploration around the following topics and questions:

Topic 1: Gender Breakdown by Major

  • Are certain majors more popular among women compared to men?
  • Are there certain major categories that are more popular amongst women?
  • Is there a correlation between the average wage and percentage of women for certain majors?

Topic 2: Women in Stem

  • Are there certain stem majors that are more popular for women?
  • What is the relationship between these majors and the average wage?
  • What is the average wage for majors that are more popular for women in stem?
