Graduate Outcomes by College Major

Introduction:

We chose to analyze the dataset "College Majors" from Five Thirty Eight. The dataset contains information on outcomes for various college majors. Select variables include median earning by major, enrollement by majors, employment rate by major, the share of women enrolled in each major, and the major category. We created a series of dashboards to analyze how outcomes differ by college major.

For our data exploration we chose to analyze the College Majors dataset. The dataset contains the breakdown of different majors across the Untied States. The data comes from the American Community Survey 2010-2012 Public Use Microdata Series. The large number of categories allowed us to investigate the popularity of certain majors across demographic groups. Additionally, we were able to investigate how outcomes differ among differnt demographic groups. We structured our data exploreation around the following topics and questions:

  • Are certain majors more popular among women compared to men?
  • Are there certain major categories that are more popular among women?
  • Are there certain STEM majors that are more popular for women?
  • Are women typically employed in high-earning jobs upon graduation?

Data Selection: We chose the college majors dataset because as current college students, we were interested in exploring the breakdown of college majors across the United States between men and women. We wanted to learn about which majors are more popular between men and women and how these differences correlate with employment rates as well as wages. We made sure to look through the data beforehand to spot any missing data, as well as lookout for any outliers.

Exploration: We explored the data by first looking at the gender breakdown by major. We made two charts to compare which majors had the largest share of women and men. To look even further we created a dashboard to explore the majors by category to observe any correlation between which categories had higher percentages of women or men. As women in Stem, we were also curious about the percentage of women in different Stem categories. We created another chart to focus on majors in Stem categories.

In addition, we wanted to answer the question of which majors/major categories had the highest wages. We created a simple bar chart to display the differences in wages across the different majors.

In order to explore the relationship between gender and college majors, we made two charts to compare which majors had the largest share of women and men. We sorted the majors by percentage of women and men, and displayed the top ten majors in each gender. We figured out that the most popular majors among women are education, childcare, and nursing. In these majors, women make up over 90% of the population. On the other hand, the engineering majors are dominated by men. The two graphs show huge gender inequality in college majors.

Then we decided to do more investigations on the college major choices among women. We made a horizontal bar chart to display the percentage of women in each major subject. Majors like psychology, education, humanities have a larger percentage of women, which aligns with the conclusion from the previous graph. Majors in the physical sciences, social sciences, and law have around 50% percent of women, they are most evenly split between men and women. However, women only make up less than 30% of math, computer science and engineering.

Female Participation in Stem Subjects

We learned from the preceding part that women have surprisingly small representations in the STEM majors like computer science and engineering. So we decided to take a further look at the women participation in each Stem major category. We found that women are most likely to major in the health field including nursing, and the fields of biology and physical science have a relatively more equal gender ratio. Once again, the engineering majors have the lowest women participation. In addition, we created an interactive chart that allows readers to see the specific data of women participation in each major.

Finally, we want to explore the majors with highest incomes, and women participating in those majors. We made a bar chart to show the top 10 majors with the highest median income. Petroleum is the most money making major, with a median income of 110k per year, but it only has 12% female students. Among the top 10 majors, 8 are engineering majors with very few female representation. The other two majors are astronomy and actuarial, which belongs to the fields of business and physical science. Astronomy has a 54% female population, and actuarial has a 44% female population. The two majors are also the only majors in top 10 earners that are slightly dominated by women. However, their median income is ranked 7th and 8th. We also implemented an interactive feature in the bar chart. The readers can hover over the bars to see the specific percentage of women in that major.
